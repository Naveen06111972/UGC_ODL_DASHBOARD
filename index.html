Base Regulations & Amendment History
Regulatory Framework
Full Title: UGC (Open and Distance Learning Programmes and Online Programmes) Regulations, 2020 — notified 4th September 2020, superseding 2017 regulations.
Scope: Applies to all HEIs offering programmes through ODL mode and fully Online mode — Universities, Deemed Universities, and Autonomous Institutions.
First Amendment (July 2021): NAAC/NIRF validity extended from 1 year to a block of 3 years for ODL/Online eligibility purposes.
Second Amendment (July 2022): Category-I HEIs allowed to offer ODL programmes without prior UGC approval. Dual degree guidelines introduced under NEP 2020.
Third Amendment (May 2024): NAAC minimum score revised from 3.26 to 3.01. Science-based programme norms tightened. AICTE NOC requirement removed for MBA/MCA for most HEIs.
UG/PG Regulations 2025: New regulation governing minimum standards for undergraduate and postgraduate degrees — multiple entry/exit, twice-a-year admissions, and cross-disciplinary admission introduced.
2024–25 onwards: DEB-ID (Distance Education Bureau ID) mandatory for all students enrolling in ODL/Online programmes, linked to ABC-ID. Valid for lifelong learning.

Institutional Eligibility — 2026 Updated Norms
Third Amendment 2024
Key Change — Third Amendment 2024: Minimum NAAC score revised from 3.26 to 3.01 on a 4-point scale for ODL mode eligibility. NIRF top-100 (University category) is now an equally valid route.
ODL Mode Eligibility (any one condition)
NAAC Route: Minimum NAAC score of 3.01 on 4-point scale (revised from 3.26 in Third Amendment, 2024).
NIRF Route: Ranked in the top 100 in the University category of NIRF at least once in two preceding cycles.
Category-I HEIs: Institutions notified as Category-I under UGC Graded Autonomy Regulations 2018 may offer ODL programmes by submitting information and affidavit — no separate prior approval required.
Online Mode Eligibility
Entitled HEIs (No prior approval needed): NAAC A+ (3.51+) or NIRF top-100 institutions can start full-fledged Online programmes under Regulation 3(B)(a).
Recognised HEIs: Others require year-wise UGC approval and must renew annually after compliance verification by submitting information and prescribed fee.
Compliance Continuity Rule
HEIs must maintain NAAC/NIRF eligibility continuously. If they fall out of compliance, they must discontinue new admissions and inform UGC. Existing enrolled students may complete their programmes.

Prohibited Programmes — Negative List 2026
Restricted Disciplines
Important: The following 16 programme categories cannot be offered under ODL or Online mode under any circumstances, regardless of the institution's NAAC/NIRF status.
Programmes Prohibited in ODL / Online Mode

Science-Based Programmes — Stricter Special Rule
Higher Bar Required: Science-based ODL programmes needing lab/hands-on work require NAAC score of 3.26+, or NIRF top-100 for at least twice in three preceding cycles — stricter than general ODL eligibility of 3.01.
LSC Condition: Can only be offered from headquarters or Learner Support Centres that have been running the same programme in conventional mode for at least 7 years.

Self-Learning Material (SLM) Norms
In-house Requirement: At least 60% of SLM/e-learning material must be developed by the HEI's own in-house faculty.
External Sourcing (40%): Remaining content may be sourced from other HEIs, Open Educational Resources (OER), and SWAYAM platform materials.
SWAYAM/NPTEL Credit Transfer: Credits from SWAYAM/NPTEL MOOCs are transferable for regular campus students under UGC Credit Framework for Online Learning Courses (SWAYAM), 2016.
Mandatory Institutional Infrastructure
LMS Platform: Functional Learning Management System — assessed on UGC parameters for tech stack compliance (non-SWAYAM platforms require separate tech assessment).
Dedicated Team: Separate content development team, online delivery team, and student support cell are mandatory. Technical support may be outsourced.
Proctored Exams: Mandatory for Online programmes. Term-end examination centre details must be listed publicly on institution website.
Transparency Mandate: Programme details, LSC addresses, enrolment data, SLM links, and exam centre info must be publicly displayed on institution website.

NCrF, Academic Bank of Credits & Dual Degree — 2025 Updates
NEP 2020 Aligned
National Credit Framework (NCrF)
NCrF Overview: 8-level unified framework integrating school, vocational, and higher education under NEP 2020. Credits from UGC-approved ODL/Online programmes are fully recognized.
Multiple Entry/Exit (UG/PG Regs 2025): Exit after Year 1 (Certificate), Year 2 (Diploma), Year 3 (UG Degree), or Year 4 (UG with Research). Re-entry allowed within 7 years through ABC.

Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) & DEB-ID
DEB-ID (New from 2024–25): Every student enrolling in ODL/Online programmes must register on UGC-DEB portal and generate a unique DEB-ID linked to their ABC-ID. Valid for lifelong learning.
Credit Transfer: Credits earned in online/ODL/regular mode may be accumulated, transferred, and redeemed across institutions within the ABC framework.
Dual Degree Rules — Updated June 2025
Permitted Combinations: (1) One full-time regular + one ODL/Online programme, (2) Two ODL/Online programmes simultaneously. Both from UGC-recognised institutions.
Condition: Class timings must not overlap. Both degrees must be at the same level — two UG degrees or two PG degrees simultaneously (not UG + PG).
Ph.D Exception: Ph.D programme cannot be pursued simultaneously with any other degree programme.
Twice-a-Year Admissions (2025): Under UG/PG Regulations 2025, colleges may offer admissions twice a year — reducing academic loss from gap years and widening ODL access.
